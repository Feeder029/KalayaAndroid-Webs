Journey Through History

Major story arcs and historical milestones

NOTE: Each milestone represents a major game chapter tied to historical events.

Act I: Formation of the Katipunan (1892–1896)

Historical Context: The Katipunan was founded on July 7, 1892 by Andrés Bonifacio and fellow revolutionaries as a secret society aiming to gain independence from Spanish colonial rule. Members recruited allies, spread revolutionary ideals, and organized resistance in secrecy.

Play as Amado Mabunga: Witness the founding of the secret society. Join the organization, deliver secret messages, and assist fellow members while avoiding the Guardia Civil. Through rescue and stealth missions, prove your loyalty and participate in the sacred initiation ritual. Unlock stealth abilities as you embrace the revolutionary cause.

Act II: The Cry of Pugad Lawin (1896)

Historical Context: The Cry of Pugad Lawin marked the open declaration of revolution against Spanish rule, symbolized by Katipuneros tearing their cedulas as an act of defiance. This event signaled the beginning of armed resistance in 1896.

Play as Alondra Dimaculangan: Experience the moment that changed history. Gather intelligence, recruit members, and strengthen revolutionary forces. Lead up to the historic act of defiance as Katipuneros tear their cedulas, declaring open war against Spanish colonial rule. Unlock powerful combat skills as the revolution officially begins.

Act III: Battle of San Juan del Monte (1896–1897)

Historical Context: The Battle of San Juan del Monte was one of the first major battles of the Philippine Revolution, where Katipuneros attempted to capture El Polvorín, a Spanish gunpowder depot. Although initially successful, Spanish reinforcements forced the revolutionaries to retreat.

Play as Mang Bernardo: Fight in one of the first major battles of the revolution. Lead assaults against Spanish forces, capture El Polvorín gunpowder depot, and secure crucial war supplies. Face overwhelming enemy reinforcements and make strategic retreats while preserving resources. Learn the cost of sacrifice and gain regenerative survival abilities.

Act IV: The Execution of José Rizal (1896)

Historical Context: Dr. José Rizal, a nationalist reformist and intellectual, was executed by the Spanish colonial government on December 30, 1896 in Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park). His execution intensified Filipino resistance and inspired many revolutionaries.

Play as Ricardo: Attempt the impossible to save a national hero. Infiltrate Fort Santiago, solve intricate puzzles, and retrieve Rizal's final writings. Despite your efforts to rescue him, witness Rizal's choice to face his fate for the nation on December 30, 1896. His martyrdom ignites the revolutionary spirit and grants you enhanced escape mobility.

Act V: The Tejeros Convention & Bonifacio's Downfall (1897)

Historical Context: The Tejeros Convention was held to establish a revolutionary government but resulted in political division between factions. The conflict eventually led to the arrest and execution of Andrés Bonifacio.

Play as Miguel Salamat: Navigate the dangerous political divisions within the revolution. Prepare crucial meetings, prevent espionage, and attempt to defend Andrés Bonifacio from opposing factions. Experience the heartbreak of internal conflict as betrayal leads to Bonifacio's tragic execution. Unlock high-risk, high-reward combat abilities born from this painful lesson.

Act VI: The Pact of Biak-na-Bato (1897)

Historical Context: The Pact of Biak-na-Bato was a truce agreement between Spanish authorities and revolutionary leaders, temporarily ending hostilities. Revolutionary leaders agreed to exile in exchange for financial compensation and promised reforms.

Play as Pedro Makabuhay: Question difficult decisions as revolutionary leaders negotiate truce with Spanish authorities. Help revolutionaries survive in Biak-na-Bato, gather resources, assist civilians, and raid enemy outposts. Struggle between continuing the fight or accepting temporary peace and exile. Gain defensive survival enhancements through resilience.

Act VII: The Return & Philippine Independence (1898)

Historical Context: In 1898, Emilio Aguinaldo returned from exile and led Filipino forces to victory against Spanish rule, culminating in the Declaration of Philippine Independence on June 12, 1898 in Kawit, Cavite. This event marked the formal proclamation of Philippine sovereignty.

Play as Juana Cruz: Witness history's triumphant moment as Emilio Aguinaldo returns from exile. Lead final military campaigns against Spanish forces—free prisoners, sabotage enemy supplies, and defeat Spanish commanders. Experience the Declaration of Philippine Independence on June 12, 1898 in Kawit, Cavite. Return to the present with a deeper understanding of sacrifice, freedom, and national identity.
